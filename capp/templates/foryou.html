The Cost of Travelling in Norway

Hi again investigator, in this page I am going to give you information related to costs in Norway that will help you solve the case of how to lower your carbon transport footprint! I personally found price ranges and estimates for all sorts of vehicles, so that you could have a complete understanding of how much your trip is going to cost. I also put the resources to which I’m referring to in each paragraph, so that you can investigate furthermore on your own, and some useful links that you might find helpful.
Think of them as clues to help both your investigation and movements in this country.

Moving by car

What about toll roads? Visit AutoPASS to know about the toll fees in Norway: https://www.autopass.no/en. AutoPASS is the electronic national toll collection system that differentiates the toll rates payments based on the type of vehicle. A summarizing table with the current toll rates by vehicle class (2025) can be found here: https://www.visitnorway.com/plan-your-trip/getting-around/toll-roads-in-norway/

Vehicle Category Description Rate Range AutoPASS discount
Rate Group 1 Vehicles ≤3.5 tonnes + M1 category NOK 5-150 20% standard discount
Rate Group 2 Vehicles >3.5 tonnes (except M1) NOK 10-590 20% discount + environmental rates
Zero-Emission Electric vehicles, hydrogen cars 30-100% discount Max 50% of standard rate
Plug-in Hybrid PHEV vehicles Reduced rates Environmental differentiation
Exempt Vehicles Motorcycles, emergency, public transport Free No payment required

Of course, the information reported here is just an estimate. Fees and discounts may vary based on the road and/or the project. For more info, check: https://www.autopass.no/en

What about parking costs? Visit the municipality webpages for the city you are staying in. Example for Oslo:
- City center: 90 NOK/hour for fossil cars, 45 NOK/hr for EVs
- Residential areas: 20-35 NOK/hour
- Shopping centers: free or ~30 NOK/hr
Public garages: 20 NOK per 15 minutes. For more info, check https://www.oslobilutleie.no and https://www.onepark.no

Taxi fares vary. According to https://www.gobytaxi.com/europe/norway : base fee 100 NOK, 14.5 NOK/km, waiting 473.1 NOK/hr.

Moving with public transport

Norway does not offer a national Public Transport system, but every region has its own. Here are some cities and ticket info:

Oslo: Single ticket (60 min, bus, tram, metro, ferry, local trains):

  • Zone 1: 40 NOK
  • Zone 2: 60 NOK
  • Zone 3: 90 NOK
  • All zones: 120 NOK

Period passes: 24h 130 NOK, 7-days 330 NOK, 30-days 814-1.304 NOK, Annual 8.664-13.872 NOK

More info: https://ruter.no/en/buying-tickets/tickets-and-fares/
